Proof
1) Laurent symmetry (no logarithmic tails).
Because JJ is analytic on the punctured plane with the only singularity (if any) at 00, it has a global Laurent expansion

J(z)=c0+∑n≥1cn (zn+z−n).(1)J(z)=c_0+\sum_{n\ge1} c_n\,(z^n+z^{-n}).\tag{1}
Since J(x)∈RJ(x)\in\mathbb R for all x>0x>0 and each zn+z−nz^n+z^{-n} is real on x>0x>0, all coefficients cnc_n in (1) are real. (No logarithmic terms can appear in an analytic Laurent expansion, so “logarithmic tails” are excluded by analyticity.)
2) Pass to the logarithmic coordinate and identify the harmonics.
Write x=eux=e^{u} with u∈Ru\in\mathbb R. Then zn+z−n=enu+e−nu=2cosh⁡(nu)z^n+z^{-n}=e^{nu}+e^{-nu}=2\cosh(nu). Define

Thus FF is a real‑analytic, even function of uu.
3) Eliminate all higher harmonics n≥2n\ge2.
The given growth bound becomes, for all u∈Ru\in\mathbb R,
F(u)≤2Kcosh⁡u.(3)F(u)\le 2K\cosh u.\tag{3}
Suppose some cm≠0c_m\neq0 with m≥2m\ge2, and let mm be maximal with that property. From (2),
F(u)=2cmcosh⁡(mu)+O(cosh⁡((m−1)u)).F(u)=2c_m\cosh(mu)+O(\cosh((m-1)u)).

Therefore,

4) Impose positivity and the unique minimizer at x=1x=1.
Restrict (4) to x>0x>0:

Then

From (5),

Near the minimizer, J′′(1)=2c1J''(1)=2c_1. The natural unit choice is to fix the curvature at the minimizer to one, J′′(1)=1J''(1)=1, which (equivalently by (6)) sets c1=12c_1=\tfrac12. Substituting c1=12c_1=\tfrac12 into (5) yields
